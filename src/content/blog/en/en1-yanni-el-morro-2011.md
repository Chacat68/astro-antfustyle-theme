> Music Without Borders: Recording melodies that transcend language and geography, listening to the heartbeat and resonance of the world.

When Greek composer Yanni set up his stage at El Morro in San Juan, Puerto Rico, the weight of history and the purity of music intertwined within this 500-year-old fortress. This 2011 live concert was not just an audiovisual feast but a cultural dialogue—amidst the waves of the Caribbean Sea and beneath the fortress walls, Yanni built a bridge connecting the past and the future with music.

El Morro is a witness to Puerto Rico's history, having withstood countless maritime invasions, now serving as the perfect stage for Yanni's music. Stone walls, sea breezes, starry skies, and stage lights together create a dreamlike space, allowing classics like "Truth of Touch," "Nightingale," and "One Man's Dream" to take on new life embraced by nature and history.

## The Charm of the Concert

### The Poetic Choice of Venue

El Morro is not just a building; it is a living piece of history. By choosing to perform here, Yanni initiated a dialogue between music and architecture, allowing modern electronic sounds to resonate with ancient stone walls. As night fell and lights illuminated the ramparts, each note seemed to tell stories spanning time and space.

### Borderless Emotional Expression

Yanni's music never needs lyrics; the melody itself is the best language. In this concert, he blended diverse cultural elements from Greek, Latin, and Eastern traditions, weaving a rich musical tapestry with instruments like piano, violin, percussion, and synthesizers. From the passionate surge of "Vertigo" to the gentle delicacy of "Felitsa," each piece narrates stories about dreams, hope, and humanity.

### The Masterful Performance of the Orchestra

Yanni's orchestra comprises top musicians from around the world, with violin, cello, keyboards, percussion, and vocals blending seamlessly. The improvisation and interaction of the live performance make each concert a unique experience. The audience can feel the chemistry and passion among the musicians—a sense of immediacy and vitality that studio recordings cannot replicate.

## Highlighted Tracks

**"Truth of Touch"** — Opening with the album's title track, its warm melody and layered arrangement guide listeners into Yanni's musical world.

**"Nightingale"** — The song of the nightingale, a delicate dialogue between violin and piano, feels like a hymn to life sung under the night sky.

**"One Man's Dream"** — One of Yanni's signature pieces, its simple yet powerful melody interprets the connection between personal dreams and universal emotions.

**"Niki Nana"** — With its strong rhythm and particularly brilliant percussion, this track showcases the wild and free-spirited side of Yanni's music.

## About Yanni

Yanni (born 1954), born in Greece, is one of the most influential contemporary New Age music composers and keyboardists. His music blends classical, jazz, rock, and world music elements, renowned for its grand arrangements, beautiful melodies, and profound emotional expression.

From the Acropolis in Athens to the Forbidden City, from the Taj Mahal in India to the Pyramids of Egypt, Yanni's journey spans world heritage sites. He uses music to connect different civilizations, conveying messages of peace and hope. This concert in Puerto Rico is yet another perfect manifestation of his philosophy to "embrace the world with music."

When music resounds between the ancient fortress and the ocean, what we hear is not just melody, but the shared emotions and dreams of humanity—the eternal pursuit of love, freedom, hope, and beauty.
