## Preface

On the journey of growth, we all experience the transformation from dependence to independence. The cover of "Kindergarten" by the Hong Kong Southern District Children's Choir presents this classic originally sung by Twins with pure, youthful voices.

Wyman Wong's lyrics act like a mirror, reflecting the child within each of us who is unwilling to grow up. When the tender voices sing, "Don't go, even if the big clock strikes, don't let go," the fear and reluctance towards growing up seem to echo right beside our ears.

This song is not only a nostalgia for childhood but also a cherishing of the innocence and attachment experienced during the process of growing up. In this fast-paced world, occasionally pausing to listen to "Kindergarten" might help us rediscover that once-innocent heart.
