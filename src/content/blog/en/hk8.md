## Preface

After experiencing many uncertainties in life, we often find the world is not as we wish. Plans get disrupted, dreams are shelved, and unmet expectations become commonplace. Everyone has faced such moments: when you push open a door full of anticipation, only to find the scenery behind it completely different from what you imagined. This sense of disparity, this feeling of powerlessness, can sometimes be suffocating and disorienting.

However, as Charmaine Fong sings in this song "If the World Turns Out Not as You Expected," perhaps the true meaning of life lies not in everything going as planned, but in how we face those moments that defy our expectations. When storms arrive, do we choose to escape, or do we meet the challenges with a humble heart? When the entire city is changing, do we go with the flow, or do we hold fast to our inner selves, never diminishing "you are you"?

This cover version by the Southern District Children's Choir, interpreting this philosophical song with pure children's voices, has a unique charm. The determination and hope conveyed through those clear voices seem to tell us: storms always have an end; you'll finally wait for what you dream of.
