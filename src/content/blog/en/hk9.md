## Preface

When Priscilla Chan's voice begins to sing, time seems to flow back to the glorious era of Cantopop. The two classics "Thousands of Songs" and "Song of the Sunset," sharing the same melody but with different lyrics, are like two sides of the same coin of life: the former is a heartfelt confession of treasuring beautiful moments at parting, while the latter is a personal soliloquy after experiencing the vicissitudes of life. On the stage of "Infinity and Beyond - Greater Bay Area," Priscilla Chan, with her signature clear and bright voice, reinterprets these two songs that carry the collective memories of countless people. This is not only a dialogue across time with Anita Mui but also a deep, affectionate look back at that golden era.

Through these two songs, we glimpse the most moving characteristic of Cantopopâ€”using the most plain words to express the full flavor of life. The lyrics by Lam Chun-keung are like a time-carving knife. The eternal hope in "Even if thousands of songs come in days to come" and the epiphany about life in "The setting sun is boundless, yet its brilliance lasts but a moment" both burst forth with soul-stirring power within simple melodies. In this era of rapid change, these classic melodies can still evoke our most sincere emotional resonance, reminding us that no matter how the world changes, the life themes of parting, growth, and perseverance are always worth singing about and remembering.
