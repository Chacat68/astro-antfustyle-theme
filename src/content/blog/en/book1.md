> "Rework" is a book by the founders of 37signals, who present an alternative way of running a business—one that is simpler, independent, and maintains high revenue. This post shares my thoughts and reflections after reading "Rework."

The "Rework" series consists of three books:

- The first discusses independently operated business models, the importance of keeping teams small, and fostering innovation.
- The second explores remote work, especially relevant during the pandemic, helping us better adapt to remote work models.
- The third addresses the current culture of excessive overtime, meetings, and common challenges in company operations.

### "Rework"

![](https://blog-1259751088.cos.ap-shanghai.myqcloud.com/20201011184505.jpg)

Most business management books will tell you: create a business plan, analyze the competition, find investors, etc. If you're looking for that kind of book, put this one back on the shelf.

This book presents a better, simpler way to succeed in business. After reading it, you'll understand why planning is actually more harmful than helpful, why you don't need outside investors, and why ignoring the competition can lead to better growth. The truth is, you need far less than you think. You don't have to be a workaholic, you don't need to hire a large team, you don't have to waste time on paperwork and meetings, and you don't even need an office. All of these are just excuses!
